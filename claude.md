## What Are AI Agents? (In Plain English)

**AI agents are autonomous digital workers that think, decide, and act on your behalf.**

Unlike basic chatbots that simply respond to questions, AI agents can:

| Traditional Chatbots | Agentic AI |
|---------------------|------------|
| Answer questions | Execute multi-step workflows |
| Require constant prompting | Work autonomously toward goals |
| Single-task focused | Coordinate across systems |
| Reactive | Proactive and self-directed |

**Think of it this way:** A chatbot is like a receptionist who answers the phone. An AI agent is like a chief of staff who handles your calendar, researches competitors, drafts communications, and executes strategic initiatives—all without being micromanaged.

## Key Concepts Every Executive Should Know

### Agentic AI Fundamentals

**Autonomy** — AI agents operate independently within defined boundaries, making decisions without human approval for routine tasks.

**Goal-Oriented Behavior** — Unlike reactive chatbots, agents work backward from objectives, planning and executing multi-step strategies.

**Tool Use** — Agents can access external systems—databases, APIs, calendars, CRMs—to gather information and take action.

**Memory & Context** — Advanced agents maintain context across conversations and learn from past interactions.

**Reasoning** — Modern AI agents can break down complex problems, weigh options, and explain their decision-making process.
